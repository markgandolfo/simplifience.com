For the first few months, what you’ll find here is material to clear up a few common mathematical misconceptions, such as:

  1. You can choose to use as little or as much number as you like.
All numbers are imaginary, and imaginary numbers aren't scary.
  1. The length of a triangle’s hypotenuse has nothing to do with squares.
The little twos in $a^2+b^2=c^2$ should not be read "squared", and you don't need a square to find the length of a triangle's long side.
  1. You can get anywhere in the universe as fast as you want.
Physics doesn't have a hard speed limit, and the limit that it does have isn't about light.

Of course, before we can address these issues we need to build up a strong foundation and an understanding of the language, which may take some work.

So the content you’ll find here is still very, very basic. We’ll work our way up to complex things, but first we need to go back and make sure that we really, truly grok the foundations.
